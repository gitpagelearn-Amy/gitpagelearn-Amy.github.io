Draft Resolution

发表于 2021-07-31

Committee: General Assembly Third Committee

Topic: Gender Equality in COVID Pandemic

Sponsors: Islamic Republic of Iran, Japan, People’s Republic of China, Singapore, The Kingdom of Saudi Arabia The Republic of India, The people’s Democratic Republic of Algeria

Signatories: The Kingdom of the Netherlands, Ukraine

​

The United Nations General Assembly Third Committee,

Recalling UN resolution A/RES/52/86,A/RES/52/93, A/RES/74/126, A/74/222, R/48/104 DEVPA, A/RES/74/128,A/RES/74/127,

Recognizing the existence of gender inequality and its negative impact on women’s rights globally,

Aware of the deterioration of gender inequality during the COVID-19 pandemic,

Appealing for specific solutions to gender inequality issues in all fields,

Affirming the majority of health workers on the front-line are women,

Recognizing among those respondents in employment when the pandemic struck, 5 percent of the women are now unemployed, compared with just 2.8 of the men,

Noticing that the COVID-19 pandemic has accelerated automation and digitization, leading to poor reemployment prospects for women,

Recognizing the rise of domestic violence in COVID pandemic,

Emphasizing the importance of solving gender inequality issues,

Reaffirming the necessity to implement suitable policies depending on the current situation of each country,

Part I Gender equality situation during the COVID-19 pandemic

  1. Realizes that women’s rights are still violated, and that the situation of gender inequality is becoming severer during the COVID-19 pandemic including the aspects below:

(1) Health

(2) Unemployment, inequality in working opportunities and income

(3) Unpaid work

(4) Gender-based violence

(5) Politics

(6) Stereotypes towards women

(7) Influence of religion

2.Notes the diversity of inequality situation in each country;

3.Encourages each country to select the suitable policies to implement domestically;

4.Calls all countries to pay attention to the inequality issue and give corresponding and specific solutions;

PART II Health gender inequality in COVID Pandemic

5.Draws attention to the lack of medical and daily necessities for front-line women health workers;

6. Calls upon governments to provide vaccination first and sufficient medical and daily necessities including protective suit, medical masks, alcohol-based hand sanitizer and so on for front-line health workers, especially considering needs of female health workers like tampons;

7.Recommends member states to ensure the provision, through the primary healthcare system, of universal access of couples and individuals to appropriate and affordable preventive services with respect to COVID pandemic;

8.Further recommends member states to expand the provision of counselling and voluntary and confidential diagnostic and treatment services for women;

9.Draws attention to the promotion of mutually respectful and equitable relations between women and men concerning health service needs under COVID Pandemic;

10.Suggests providing more accessible, available and affordable primary healthcare services of high quality including sexual, reproductive, vaccination healthcare through significant increase in investment in social and medical infrastructure;

11.Suggests facilitating coordination and cooperation among governments, departments, agencies, offices and organizations to build a well-bedded system for promoting women’s healthy welfare under COVID pandemic;

12.Suggests increasing participation of women in medical research such as medical trials;

PART II Unemployment

13.Proposes the convening of another Global Leaders’ Meeting on gender equality and women’s empowerment in 2025 to contribute to women’s development in post-pandemic era;

14.Suggests implementing social and income protection mechanisms for alternative employment models to ensure appropriate coverage for all workers in the formal and informal economy, with special attention to essential workers, part-time workers and the self-employed;

15.Recommends to design and test innovative solutions such as portable benefits which workers could carry across multiple jobs;

16.Suggests providing vocational skill training, online courses equally for women and men during COVID pandemic;

17.Calls on governments to eliminate regulatory, legislative, and sociocultural barriers that prevent equal access of women to financial products and services;

18.Calls on governments to make good use of domestic financial institutions, such as banks, and encourage charity fund organizations or companies to provide financial assistance for women in urgent need and ensure women’s access to financial services and products;

19.Suggests adopting gender-responsive budgeting informed by gender impact assessment to ensure that post-pandemic recovery measures foster a gender-inclusive workforce;

20.Calls for investment in supporting infrastructure such as childcare, parental leave and education, in order to increase women’s confidence to reenter the labour market after pandemic;

21.Calls on governments to provide unemployment benefits for fired women, in order to:

(1) Help individuals who have lost their job to sustain a necessary consumption level;

(2) Help creating a more leisurely period of time, so that there will be a bigger possibility for unemployed women to find suitable and sustainable jobs and avoid entering an endless loop of unemployment;

(3) Encourage shifting the business of companies that have been hit severely by the pandemic to those that have been rapidly developing for the stimulation of COVID-19;

22.Suggests instructing domestic citizens to modify conventional stereotypes towards women;

23.Recommends countries to refer to suggestions below when introducing or expanding the use of gender quota, including but not limited to:

(1) The main goal is to ensure that women can overcome obstacles in entering male dominated industries;

(2) Conducting trials in administrative field to which gender quota has contributed more, in order to examine the effects of gender-quota on both positive and negative sides;

(3) Deciding domestic gender quota standard according to countries’ actual situation;

(4) Considering women’s will and how to make up for the novel gap caused by the transfer of women’s proportions labour force;

PART III Unpaid Work

24.Calls on governments to measure the true value of women’s unpaid care and domestic work and its estimated contribution to the economy;

25.Suggests increasing the unpaid work for males who can spare time on family work;

26.Strongly suggests raising the public awareness of gender imbalance in responsibility for domestic care and housework, especially under COVID pandemic;

27.Suggests engaging with men and masculinity as an integral part of securing a positive shift in norms and behaviours about unpaid work;

28.Suggests taking action to correct social norms that undermine women’s agency and access to opportunity for social and economic empowerment;

29.Suggests recognizing and valuing the hidden work that women are doing through gender-disaggregated data and gender-sensitive programming;

30.Suggests redistributing care work between households and the state in the form of government-provided care services for children, people with disabilities and the elderly;

PART IV Gender-based violence

31.Calls on countries to affirm the severity of gender-based violence since gender-based violence apparently threatens the physical health of women, as well as their psychological health, doubling the psychological burden of women caused by COVID pandemic;

32.Calls upon the resolution of ending violence by gender of all forms, including but not limited to raping, sexual harassment, etc.;

33.Draws attention to new forms of gender-based violence, cyber violence towards women for example;

34.Calls on countries to be aware of the different issues each country faces to resolve gender-based violence, including but not limited to cultures and religions;

35.Suggests creating or strengthening institutional mechanisms so that women and girls can report acts of violence against them in a safe and confidential environment, free from the fear of penalties or retaliation, and file charges;

36.Calls on countries to ensure that women with disability have access to information and services in the field of violence against women during and after COVID pandemic;

37.Recommends the methods below to reduce gender-based violence:

(1) Advocating education on gender equality and methods for boycotting gender-based violence;

(2) Establishing laws against gender-based violence, with specified definition of gender-based violence and the corresponding penalties to the perpetrators;

(3) Enhancing the existing mechanisms for women to combat gender-based violence;

(4) Providing hotline and shelter services for women who suffer from gender-based violence and need assistance and consultation;

(5) Collecting data on gender-based violence as a reference of implementation of corresponding policies;

(6) Opposing extremists’ misinterpretation and use of certain religious doctrines;

(7) Strengthening the online censorship of contents showing discrimination and aggression towards women.
