Position Paper

发表于 2021-07-31

Delegate: Zhao Jie
Delegation: Japan
Committee: The Third Committee of the United Nations General Assembly
Topic: Gender equality in COVID pandemic
School: Jinling High School

Introduction

The patriarchal clan society led to gender discrimination as most men had an advantage over women in physical strength, however, gender inequality still exists these days. Since the spread of the COVID-19, some situations even have made it more serious. For example, many companies tend to fire female employees to ensure their profits. This has caused dissatisfaction among many people.
As the builders and contributors of society, women have the right to fight for and take back the opportunities they should have. Moreover, eliminating gender discrimination can improve social civilization, increase productivity, and promote the emergence of more talents. So it is important for us to help women and take actions to eliminate gender inequality.

Past actions

The United Nations passed The Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women at the General Assembly on December 18, 1979, and it entered into force in September 1981. It is an international convention on the rights of women and promotes the feminist movement through international treaties. It is also an important international human rights instrument formulated by the United Nations to eliminate discrimination against women and strive for gender equality. The convention establishes rules to protect women’s rights in politics, law, work, education, medical services, business activities and family relations. UN Women claimed that many women’s industries were hit hard by the COVID-19. Women are increasingly facing the difficulty of making a living. There will be more than 47 million women and children living below the poverty line in the world. At the same time, UN Women called on all the countries to strengthen solidarity and cooperation to promote gender equality and women’s empowerment.
Since Japanese companies use the annual merit-based wage system for their wages, companies are tend to recruit male employees. In order to solve the serious gender discrimination of Japanese companies in recruiting employees, Japan has banned filling in gender and pasting photos on resumes.

Position of Japan

Due to the influence of Japanese traditional culture, there is still serious gender discrimination in Japanese society. The COVID-19 has made gender discrimination harming Japanese women’s lives more, especially those who living at the bottom of society. Gender inequality has no benefits to the development and civilization progress, so Japan supports the elimination of gender discrimination. As a developed country, Japan will actively take equal rights measures.
On December 18, the World Economic Forum (WEF) released the “2018 Global Gender Difference Report”. Among the 149 countries surveyed, Japan ranked 110th (with a total score of 0.662) and was still the last place in the G7 group. The main reason for Japan’s low ranking is its low scores in the economic and political sectors. Excessive income gap between men and women (103, score 0.527) and large gap in the number of men and women in management positions (129, score 0.152) have a greater impact. In the education and health care parts, Japan has achieved a high score of nearly full score.
According to this report, Japan will investigate if companies treat male and female employees differently and introduce policies to narrow the wage gap between male and female employees to ensure women’s income. At the same time, the government will stipulate the ratio of male and female employees in enterprises to solve the problem of high unemployment among women. Japan will also encourage women to participate in politics and improve women’s social status. At the same time, Japan will maintain equality in education and health. We will also fight against those who think that women should do housework to take care of children and the elderly for unpaid work in home. Develop social discussions on a larger scale and awaken people’s awareness to achieve gender equality is the result that beneficial to the public.
