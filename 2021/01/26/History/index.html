History

History

ROME PERIOD

  • Early civilization

About 700 B.C., the Celts, who lived in Western Europe, continued to move into the British Isles. One of them was called the Britons, which the name Britain probably came from.

  • Roman invasion

In 43 A.D., Britain transformed it into a province of the Roman Empire. In 409, the Roman garrison was forced to withdraw from Britain, and Rome’s rule over Britain came to an end.

MEDIEVAL TIMES

  • Anglo Saxon

At the beginning of the 5th century, after the withdrawal of the Romans, the Anglo Saxons and the Jutes conquered Britain. The angles called Britain “angland”.(Homophonic English, the origin of the English name)

At the beginning of the 7th century, the land became private property, and aristocrats, landowners, dependent farmers and slaves appeared, which was generally considered to be the beginning of the feudal process in British society. Christianity was introduced into England at the end of the 6th century.

  • Danish invasion

During the Danish occupation, the process of British feudalization accelerated.

  • Norman Conquest

William, Duke of Normandy in France, led his army to invade in 1066. In October of the same year, he entered London and was crowned as King William 1.

The powerful monarchy established by William after his conquest played a positive role in consolidating the feudal order. (Great Charter)

  • Watt Taylor Uprising

In 1380, King Richard II increased the poll tax in order to collect the expenses of the Hundred Years’ War, which led to the break of uprising in May 1381, organized by the bricklayer watt Taylor. Although the uprising failed, it cracks down the feudal serfdom in England. In the 15th century, the feudal lord class changed– new aristocrats appeared, and capitalist management was adopted.

  • Hundred Years’ War

Britain and France fought off and on for 116 years during the Black Death epidemic. Under the double attack of war and epidemic disease, British economy suffered a lot, but it also made England’s nationalism rise. By the end of the war, Britain had embarked on the road of centralization.

PRIMITIVE ACCUMULATION OF CAPITA

Enclosure movement, overseas plunder and trade are important methods of British capital primitive accumulation. England and Wales merged in 1536. From 1577 to 1580, the circumnavigation shocked Europe. In 1588, Britain defeated the Spanish Armada.

AUTOCRATIC MONARCHY

In 1603, King James VI of Scotland carried on the British throne. From the second half of the 16th century to the first half of the 17th century, the capitalist economy developed rapidly.

The Glorious Revolution broke out in 1688-1689 and the bill of rights announced laid the base of British constitutional monarchy. In 1707, it merged with Scotland.

THE RENAISSANCE

Compared with the European continent, renaissance took place later in Britain but came first. In this period, Shakespeare, bacon and Harvey are the most outstanding in the fields of art, literature and science.

INDUSTRIAL REVOLUTION

Britain is the first industrialized country in the world. It was the first to make many scientific discoveries and inventions.

COLONIAL EXPANSION

After the merger of Ireland in 1801, Britain was named the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland. It became the largest global colonial empire in history, taking up a quarter of the world’s population and land area at that time. After the kingdom of Spain in the 16th century, it was known as “the sun never sets the Empire”.

In the middle of the 19th century, Britain launched two Opium Wars to invade China, occupied Hong Kong Island and participated in the suppression of the Taiping Revolution. In 1876, after Queen Victoria was crowned Queen of India, Britain was also known as the British Empire.

WORLD WAR

After the 1870s, Britain lost its industrial monopoly. In the early 20th century, Germany became its competitor. Britain gave up the “splendid isolation” foreign policy pursued in the 19th century to form the agreement with France and Russia after 1907. In August 1914, First World War broke out. In 1917, Britain defeated Germany’s “unrestricted submarine warfare” and maintained its sea control power.

In September 1939, World War II broke out and Britain declared war on Germany. Churchill came to power in April 1940. From July to September 1940, the British air force attacked the enemy in the “Air Battle of Britain” launched by Germany. In June 1944, British and American troops landed in Normandy. France and Germany surrendered on May 8, 1945. Yalta conference was held from 4 to 11 in February, 1945.

POSTWAR PERIOD

  • Britain participated in drawing up and accepting the Marshall Plan in 1947 and received a large amount of aid from the United States and its economy gradually recovered.

  • It joined the European Community in January 1973.

  • After the 1979 election, the conservative party, Ms. Thatcher, was in power.

  • After the decolonization in the 20th century, British Overseas Territories gradually became independent, and most of them formed a peace organization with Britain–the Commonwealth of Nations.

  • On September 18, 2014, 4 million permanent residents of Scotland held a referendum to decide whether Scotland would remain in the UK.

  • On October 28, 2016, the UK was elected to the UN Human Rights Council for a term from 2017 to 2019.

  • On January 31, 2020, Britain officially left Europe and ended its 47 year EU membership.
