Main point: Willpower is a kind of physiological instinct, which is like pressure, protects us from self harm through continuous evolution. When the brain and body work at the same time ,we can overcome impulse.

The amygdala uses the advantage of being in the middle of the brain to quickly transmit information: the adrenal gland releases pressure hormones, the energy stored in the form of fat and sugar enters the blood vessels and liver, which is then transported to the muscles and lungs inhales enough air.

Heart rate variability Physical willpower “storage”

Heart rate variability reflects the degree of willpower. For example, people who abstain from alcohol may continue to be sober if they see an increase in the variability of heart rate. In contrate, if the variability of heart rate decreases, they may be impulsive enough to drink again.

Eat more unprocessed food , food made of plants and less junk food.

Live in a place with good air quality.

Reduce the rate of respiratory (4-6 times per minute).

Keep doing sports which you want to do.

Get enough sleep.

Relax.

What is willpower? & Why is willpower important?

发表于 2021-01-30

MAIN POINT: Willpower is actually three forces called “I do not want to do”, “I want to do” and “What I want to do” .They work together to make ourselves better.

Principles of Neurology

The prefrontal cortex (a kind of grey matters) makes us to do things which is more difficult but right .

The stronger the cell activity, the stronger the ability to take action and resist temptation.

I don’t want to: control your impulsivity
What I want to do: deal with boring, difficult and stressful work
I want to: record goals, desires and what you really want

Willpower challenge (know yourself properly)

We use cookie monsters, critics and procrastinators to describe people who enjoy pleasure in good time, like to complain or always do not want to work respectively.

When we are aware of the existence of the negative self, it is actually helpful to wake up the wise self (willpower). It’s wrong to want to get rid of the negative self (primitive impulse). Because getting rid of desire leads to depression and getting rid of fear leads to injury.

The key to success
  • Learn how to use primitive instinct instead of resisting.
  • Know that the self-control system and survival instinct may conflict or cooperate with each other (want to buy something but give up for its high price).
  • The brain can be reshaped according to the requirements of people. With a certain amount of training, the density of certain areas in the brain will increase. As a result, there will be more grey matter.
**Training methods (Meditation) **

Meditation can improve self-control and attention. At the same time, it can also reduce pressure, restrain the impulse and improve your self-awareness ability.

(at least 5 minutes a day, focus on the breath itself)

Summary

There are several themselves in the brain of modern people. They compete with each other, trying to control our ideas. Each willpower challenge is a self game. The purpose of doing the willpower challenge is to let a better self control our bodies. In this way, we can choose to do right things.

History

发表于 2021-01-26

History

ROME PERIOD

  • Early civilization

About 700 B.C., the Celts, who lived in Western Europe, continued to move into the British Isles. One of them was called the Britons, which the name Britain probably came from.

  • Roman invasion

In 43 A.D., Britain transformed it into a province of the Roman Empire. In 409, the Roman garrison was forced to withdraw from Britain, and Rome’s rule over Britain came to an end.

MEDIEVAL TIMES

  • Anglo Saxon

At the beginning of the 5th century, after the withdrawal of the Romans, the Anglo Saxons and the Jutes conquered Britain. The angles called Britain “angland”.(Homophonic English, the origin of the English name)

At the beginning of the 7th century, the land became private property, and aristocrats, landowners, dependent farmers and slaves appeared, which was generally considered to be the beginning of the feudal process in British society. Christianity was introduced into England at the end of the 6th century.

  • Danish invasion

During the Danish occupation, the process of British feudalization accelerated.

  • Norman Conquest

William, Duke of Normandy in France, led his army to invade in 1066. In October of the same year, he entered London and was crowned as King William 1.

The powerful monarchy established by William after his conquest played a positive role in consolidating the feudal order. (Great Charter)

  • Watt Taylor Uprising

In 1380, King Richard II increased the poll tax in order to collect the expenses of the Hundred Years’ War, which led to the break of uprising in May 1381, organized by the bricklayer watt Taylor. Although the uprising failed, it cracks down the feudal serfdom in England. In the 15th century, the feudal lord class changed– new aristocrats appeared, and capitalist management was adopted.

  • Hundred Years’ War

Britain and France fought off and on for 116 years during the Black Death epidemic. Under the double attack of war and epidemic disease, British economy suffered a lot, but it also made England’s nationalism rise. By the end of the war, Britain had embarked on the road of centralization.

PRIMITIVE ACCUMULATION OF CAPITA

Enclosure movement, overseas plunder and trade are important methods of British capital primitive accumulation. England and Wales merged in 1536. From 1577 to 1580, the circumnavigation shocked Europe. In 1588, Britain defeated the Spanish Armada.

AUTOCRATIC MONARCHY

In 1603, King James VI of Scotland carried on the British throne. From the second half of the 16th century to the first half of the 17th century, the capitalist economy developed rapidly.

The Glorious Revolution broke out in 1688-1689 and the bill of rights announced laid the base of British constitutional monarchy. In 1707, it merged with Scotland.

THE RENAISSANCE

Compared with the European continent, renaissance took place later in Britain but came first. In this period, Shakespeare, bacon and Harvey are the most outstanding in the fields of art, literature and science.

INDUSTRIAL REVOLUTION

Britain is the first industrialized country in the world. It was the first to make many scientific discoveries and inventions.

COLONIAL EXPANSION

After the merger of Ireland in 1801, Britain was named the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland. It became the largest global colonial empire in history, taking up a quarter of the world’s population and land area at that time. After the kingdom of Spain in the 16th century, it was known as “the sun never sets the Empire”.

In the middle of the 19th century, Britain launched two Opium Wars to invade China, occupied Hong Kong Island and participated in the suppression of the Taiping Revolution. In 1876, after Queen Victoria was crowned Queen of India, Britain was also known as the British Empire.

WORLD WAR

After the 1870s, Britain lost its industrial monopoly. In the early 20th century, Germany became its competitor. Britain gave up the “splendid isolation” foreign policy pursued in the 19th century to form the agreement with France and Russia after 1907. In August 1914, First World War broke out. In 1917, Britain defeated Germany’s “unrestricted submarine warfare” and maintained its sea control power.

In September 1939, World War II broke out and Britain declared war on Germany. Churchill came to power in April 1940. From July to September 1940, the British air force attacked the enemy in the “Air Battle of Britain” launched by Germany. In June 1944, British and American troops landed in Normandy. France and Germany surrendered on May 8, 1945. Yalta conference was held from 4 to 11 in February, 1945.

POSTWAR PERIOD

  • Britain participated in drawing up and accepting the Marshall Plan in 1947 and received a large amount of aid from the United States and its economy gradually recovered.

  • It joined the European Community in January 1973.

  • After the 1979 election, the conservative party, Ms. Thatcher, was in power.

  • After the decolonization in the 20th century, British Overseas Territories gradually became independent, and most of them formed a peace organization with Britain–the Commonwealth of Nations.

  • On September 18, 2014, 4 million permanent residents of Scotland held a referendum to decide whether Scotland would remain in the UK.

  • On October 28, 2016, the UK was elected to the UN Human Rights Council for a term from 2017 to 2019.

  • On January 31, 2020, Britain officially left Europe and ended its 47 year EU membership.

Garbage classification

发表于 2021-01-25

My friends and I searched some information about garbage classification. Here it is.

Definition

Garbage classification generally refers to a series of activities, including storage classification throwing and transportation according to certain regulations or standards, so as to transform it into public resources. It has social, economic and ecological benefits.

Why do we need to do refuse classification?

With the development of the technology, the environment is changing for the worse. White pollution, greenhouse effect, desertification and so on are all endangering our lives. So the classification of waste is necessary. In the past, people just threw the rubbish into the sea. It imperils the safety of the marine animals like polar bears. Second, refuse classification can greatly reduce land occupation because some substances in waste are not easy to degrade. Third, turning waste into treasure is helpful to improve the rate of recycling efficiency.

How to separate garbage

The recycling is divided into five parts, which includes the waste paper, plastic, glass, metal and cloth.

  • The waste paper covers newspapers, magazines, books, and all kinds of wrapping paper. It is necessary to mention that tissue is not included because it dissolves in water easily.

  • The plastic covers plastic bags, plastic package, plastic tableware, plastic toothbrush, plastic cup and so on.

  • The glass mainly covers glass bottles, broken glass. However, the mirror is not included.

  • The metal covers different kinds of cans.

  • The cloth covers waste clothes, tablecloth, schoolbag and shoes.

If these things can be recycled, the resources will be saved.

  • The other garbage covers tissue, shell, and dust. If they are buried properly, they can reduce the pollution of water and soil.

  • The kitchen garbage covers bones, pericarp, and leftovers. After dealing with them by biotechnology, every tons of them can produce 0.6-0.7 tons of organic fertilizer.

  • The harmful garbage covers batteries, fluorescent tubes, bulbs, mercurial thermometer, overdue pedicure and so on. All of them contain toxic substances, so they are recycled specially.

How to prove the sum of interior angles of triangles equals to 180 degrees

Draw line DE which passes point C and parallels to line AB
∵DE//AB
∴∠B=∠1,∠A=∠2
∵point D,C,E are all on the straight line DE
∴∠DCE=180°
∴∠A+∠B+∠BCA=180°
∴The sum of interior angles of triangles equals to 180°

∵CE//AB

∴∠A=∠1,∠B=∠2

∵point B,C,D are all on the straight line BD

∴∠BCD=180°

∴∠A+∠B+∠BCA=180°

∴The sum of interior angles of triangles equals to 180°

How do microphones work

发表于 2020-10-04

When a person speak or sing to a microphone, the sound wave makes the diaphragm of the microphone vibrate. So the coil which is connected to diaphragm cuts the magnetic induction line in the magnetic field and generates current. Moreover,the magnitude of the current depends on the loudness of the sound. In Physics,we call that electromagnetic induction.
